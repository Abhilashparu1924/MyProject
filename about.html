Building Your Professional Portfolio Website: Final Project Report

Introduction

Stephen R. Covey's "The 7 Habits of Highly Effective People" is used in the professional portfolio website project to create a dynamic platform that showcases accomplishments, skills, and potential. The goal of the project is to improve personal effectiveness and professionalism by incorporating Covey's ideas. The final project covers the implementation of Habits 5, 6, and 7, finishing the portfolio with detailed feedback, collaboration, and reflection.

Objectives

  • Plan and build a website for your portfolio.
  • Use the 7 Habits of Highly Effective People to improve yourself and your career.
  • Professionally and logically, show off your skills, projects, and accomplishments.
  • Think about how you learned and how you used Covey's habits.

Assignment Breakdown

Habit 1: Be Proactive

Task 1: Find and choose a platform or tool for building websites. Explain your choice in a few words, focusing on how proactive you are in getting the skills or information you need to use the platform well.

  • Platform Selection:
    • Chosen Platform: Made from scratch with JavaScript, HTML, and CSS
    • Rationale:
      • Complete Control and Customization: Gives you full control over design and functionality, letting you add advanced features like animations and interactivity.
      • Skill Enhancement: Building the site from scratch helps you learn how to code and gets you ready for more difficult projects in the future.
      • Performance and Optimization: This department ensures that code and assets are optimized, which boosts site speed and SEO.
  • Proactive Skill Acquisition:
    • Educational Pursuits: Signed up for online classes that teach HTML, CSS, and JavaScript.
    • Practical Application: Did small projects to put their skills to use in the real world.
    • Community Engagement: I joined communities for web developers to get feedback and learn more.

Habit 2: Begin with the End in Mind

Task 2: Make a project plan for your website that includes content, goals, and the people you want to reach.

  • Project Goals:
    • Primary Goal: Make an online portfolio of your web development work.
    • Secondary Goal: Make a personal brand to get potential employers and work partners interested in you.
  • Target Audience:
    • Primary Audience: Managers of hiring, tech recruiters, and HR staff
    • Secondary Audience: Other developers, people who work in the same field, and possible collaborators.
  • Critical Sections:
    • Home: A professional headshot, an introduction, and a short work history.
    • About: A lot of details about education, work history, and skills.
    • Projects: Showcase of projects with screenshots, code, and links to demos.
    • Skills: An overview of hard and soft skills.
    • Blog: Articles about building websites
    • Contact: Email for business, a contact form, and links to social media.
  • Content Plan:
    • Visual Design: Sleek, modern, responsive design with fonts and color schemes that go well together.
    • Content Strategy: Writing that is clear, to the point, and focused on strengths and accomplishments.
    • SEO and Analytics: Relevant keywords and metadata, as well as analytics tools to keep an eye on how well the site is doing.

Habit 3: Put First Things First

Task 3: Plan out the tasks you need to do for your project and put them on a timeline in order of importance and due date.

  • Timeline Development and Task Prioritization: To ensure the website is finished well, you need to set priorities for tasks and know how to use your time well. This list of things that need to be done from July 23, 2024, to August 4, 2024, is organized by how important they are and how long they are expected to take.
  • First Configuration and Design (July 23, 2024 - July 27, 2024)
    • Task: Finish the website's overall design, layout, structure, and user interface.
    • Rationale: Designing a website is important because it decides how nice it looks and how easy it is for people to use. A well-thought-out design ensures that the user has a pleasant and easy time.
    • Time Allocation: 5 days
  • Content Creation (July 28 - August 2, 2024)
    • Task: Save the changes you made to your website's Home, About, Projects, Skills, Blog, and Contact pages.
    • Rationale: When showcasing your professional skills and achievements, it's important to include relevant information. This part is all about being clear and interesting about what you know and have done.
    • Time Allocation: 6 days
  • Technical Implementation (August 3 - August 7, 2024)
    • Task: Build the site with HTML, CSS, and JavaScript and bring the design and content to life.
    • Rationale: In this phase, the website is coded, and interactive parts are added. This makes sure that the website works well on all devices. Making sure that features look good and work well is a very important technical task.
    • Time Allocation: 5 days
  • SEO and Analytics Setup (August 8 - August 9, 2024)
    • Task: For search engines to find the website, it should have software that watches what people do on it (analytics).
    • Rationale: This step is very important if you want the website to work better and reach more people. SEO techniques help people find websites, and analytics tell us how people use websites, which helps us think of new features.
    • Time Allocation: 2 days
  • Testing and Quality Assurance (August 10 - August 12, 2024)
    • Task: You should test the website carefully and fix any bugs to make sure it works perfectly.
    • Rationale: Testing helps to find bugs, compatibility issues, and performance bottlenecks. Quality assurance makes sure that users have a good experience when using the website.
    • Time Allocation: 3 days
  • Final Review and Launch (August 13 - August 14, 2024)
    • Task: Make sure everything is done, check for mistakes, and put the website online.
    • Rationale: The last step is to check and fix any last-minute problems before putting the site online. This will give users the best experience possible.
    • Time Allocation: 2 days

Development Process

  • Initial Planning:
    • Task Definition: Clearly outlines the goals and steps needed to finish the project.
    • Platform and Tool Selection: Picked a coding-based approach (JavaScript, HTML, CSS) to have more control and learn more.
  • Design:
    • Wireframing: Visualized the layout and user flow.
    • UI/UX Design: Focused on making things easy to use and look good.
  • Content Creation:
    • Writing: I wrote content that showed off my skills and accomplishments.
    • SEO Optimization: Focused on important keywords and best practices to improve visibility.
  • Development:
    • Front-End Development: Used HTML, CSS, and JavaScript to build the site.
    • Responsive Design: Made sure the site works well on devices of all sizes.
  • Testing:
    • Cross-Browser Testing: Made sure the site worked on all major browsers.
    • Bug Fixes: Fixed technical issues to make sure everything worked well.

Challenges and Solutions

  • Time Management: Managed a tight timeline by making a detailed project plan and sticking to deadlines.
  • Technical Difficulties: Fixed problems with the code by looking into them and using resources from the community.
  • SEO Optimization: I found out how to use metadata and keywords to improve search rankings.

Outcome and Reflection

The professional portfolio website was successfully completed by following the project plan, staying true to Covey's habits, and dealing with problems as they came up. The final product meets all the goals that were set, and the site is a good way to show off your skills and accomplishments. Building this website helped us learn important technical skills and taught us a lot about planning, keeping track of time, and being committed to personal and professional growth.
